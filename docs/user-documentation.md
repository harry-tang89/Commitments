# User Documentation

## Introduction

Commitments helps you create personal goals, track deadlines, and share commitments with other users. You can use it as a guest in your browser or sign in to save your data permanently.

## Getting Started

Open the app in your browser. The home page is the main entry point.

If you are not signed in:

- You can still create commitments
- Your commitments are stored only in your browser
- They are not saved to the server until you create an account or sign in

If you are signed in:

- Commitments are saved to your account
- You can edit, complete, delete, and share them

## Creating an Account

1. Open the `Create account` page.
2. Enter your email address.
3. Select `Send verification code`.
4. Check your email inbox and enter the 6-digit code.
5. Enter an optional username.
6. Enter your date of birth.
7. Create and confirm your password.
8. Select `Continue`.

After registration, you are signed in automatically.

## Signing In

1. Open the `Login` page.
2. Enter your email address and password.
3. Optionally enable `Remember Me`.
4. Select `Sign In`.

If your credentials are correct, you will be redirected to the home page.

## Resetting a Forgotten Password

1. Open `Forgotten your password?`
2. Enter your email address.
3. Continue to the date-of-birth verification step.
4. Enter the same date of birth used during registration.
5. Enter and confirm a new password.
6. Return to the sign-in page.

## Creating a Commitment

From the home page:

1. Enter a title.
2. Select a deadline date.
3. Optionally add a description.
4. Optionally select a category.
5. Select `Create Commitment`.

Available categories:

- General
- Study
- Health
- Travel

## Managing Commitments on the Home Page

The home page displays:

- `My Commitments`
- `Completed Commitments`
- Overdue filters
- Priority comparison tools

Depending on your account state, you can:

- Create commitments quickly
- Edit commitments
- Mark commitments complete
- Recover completed commitments back to active
- View members
- Add members

## Viewing All Commitments

Signed-in users can select `View All` to open the full commitments page.

This page separates commitments into:

- `Owned by Me`
- `Joined by Invitation`

Each card shows:

- Title
- Description
- Deadline date
- Status
- Progress percentage

## Editing a Commitment

To update a commitment:

1. Open the commitment.
2. Select `Edit`.
3. Update the title, description, deadline date, or category.
4. Save your changes.

You can edit commitments you own and commitments shared with you.

## Marking a Commitment Complete

To change progress status:

1. Open the commitment card.
2. Select `Mark Complete`.

If a commitment is already complete, the system may offer a way to change it back to active.

## Deleting a Commitment

To delete a commitment:

1. Open the commitment card.
2. Select `Delete`.

Only the owner of a commitment can delete it.

## Sharing a Commitment

You can share a commitment with another registered user.

To add a member:

1. Open the member-management option for a commitment.
2. Enter the member's email address.
3. Submit the form.

Shared members can view and edit the commitment.

## Viewing Commitment Members

For shared commitments, the members screen shows:

- The owner
- All collaborators
- The total number of members

## Leaving a Shared Commitment

If another user has added you to a commitment, you can leave it.

Important rule:

- Owners cannot leave their own commitments

## Guest Mode

If you create commitments before signing in:

- They are stored locally in your browser
- They may disappear if browser storage is cleared
- After sign-in, the app can sync them into your account

## Settings

The app includes a `Settings` page accessible from the top navigation. Use it to manage available app preferences.

## Tips for Best Use

- Use clear titles so commitments are easy to scan
- Add descriptions when the task needs context
- Set realistic deadline dates
- Use categories to keep different kinds of commitments organized
- Share commitments only with people who need to collaborate on them
